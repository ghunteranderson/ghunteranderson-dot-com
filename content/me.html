Having practiced since 5th grade, I was excited to start my first development job as a college sophomore. Southwest Airlines contracted me to maintain a legacy internal safety reporting application. It was not glamorous but seldom is a junior developers first assignment. After eight months, my consulting company promoted me to Technical Lead. The next two years were spent working beside new developers helping them succeed.

Since then, I've had the opportunity to work with a variety of teams, technologies, and companies from legacy monoliths to decoupled microservices, young teams to experienced teams, start ups to corporations, contractors to employees, scrum, waterfall, scrumfall, you name it. My goal is to use those experiences to contribute back to the developer community in the same way it has contributed to me.

If you want to chat or work together, reach out to me on LinkedIn or Twitter.
